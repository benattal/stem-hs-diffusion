Now to model humans for digital devices is a question of how we put something as complex as a human body into numbers that can be quantified by a computer.

This is actually no simple task and researchers all over the world (us included) are working on this.

One common way to do this is to define a set of visually distinct set of keypoints on the face (such as the iris, tip of the nose, corner of the mouth). The same thing can be done for the body by defining the joints like the elbow, knee, etc.

Another way is inspired by human anatomy, where the body is represented through a set of bones that are connected through joints (like the elbow). The pose can then be defined through the rotation of these joints.

For a more detailed representation of the skin, 3D meshes are often used. These meshes are essentially big networks of many points (or so called vertices) connected by triangles. With this mesh, body shape and skin deformations can be more accurately modelled.

To get the realistic and high definition appearance of virtual characters in places such as video games, animators use something called textures. These textures are color maps that define the final appearance of the digital humans and add details such as hair, eyebrows and clothing.

Often, movie and video game animators use a combination of these techniques to animate their virtual characters.

Have you guys played Fortnite? The motion animations (like the emotes) are represented with skeletons, and the skins are details which give you a unique character appearance.
