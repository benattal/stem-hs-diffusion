Build through three steps. Step 1: "So we have spatial blur: replace each pixel with the average of its neighbors in the image." Step 2: "And temporal blur: replace each pixel with the average of the same location across multiple frames." Step 3: "Both follow the same pattern: replace each value with a weighted combination of nearby values. This is called filtering. And it turns out filtering is one of the most powerful ideas in all of computer vision — almost everything we're going to look at today, including edge detection and deep learning, is built on this single operation."
